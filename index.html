About Me

I’m a second year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Purdue University, Indianapolis working in machine learning and computer visualization under the direction of Dr. Mukhopadhyay and Dr. Fang. My work focuses on developing new methods in adaptive machine learning to better understand the environment and integrate human feedback into the system.

My research interests are in machine learning, natural language processing, adversarial robustness in social networks and data ethics.

Thesis: Interactive Machine Learning - A Multimodal Approach

The goal is to drive advances in adaptive machine learning by predicting successful metrics in educational use cases. Using natural language processing, extraction of audio video features, and active learning recommendations, the algorithm bridges the gap between human centric and machine centric approaches.
